Salvador Dalí

Dalí was a skilled draftsman, best known for the striking
and bizarre images in his surrealist work. His painterly
skills are often attributed to the influence of Renaissance
masters. His best-known work, The Persistence of Memory,
was completed in August 1931. Dalí’s expansive artistic
répertoire included film, sculpture, and photography, in
collaboration with a range of artists in a variety of media.
